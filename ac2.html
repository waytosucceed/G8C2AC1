Theme/ What the Author wanted to convey through the story

The passage portrays a scene of intense heat and drought, where the characters, particularly Garnet and her family, are struggling to cope with the harsh conditions. Through the vivid descriptions of the scorching weather, the parched landscape, and the futile anticipation of rain, the author conveys themes of resilience, frustration, and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Garnet's repeated frustration with the unrelenting heat and her desperate plea for rain symbolize humanity's struggle against forces beyond its control. Despite her anger and despair, Garnet continues with her daily tasks, demonstrating resilience and determination in the face of hardship. Similarly, her father's resignation to the reality of the situation reflects the acceptance of circumstances that cannot be changed.

The author also highlights the importance of family and community support during difficult times. Garnet's interaction with her mother and brother, as well as her routine tasks like setting the table and fetching water, emphasize the bonds of love and responsibility that hold the family together despite the challenges they face.

Overall, the passage serves as a reminder of the resilience of the human spirit, the importance of perseverance in the face of adversity, and the enduring power of family and community support. It encourages readers to find strength and solace in their connections with others, even in the most trying of circumstances.

*** trying circumstances is used to talk about a difficult situation or experience***
